About

Curriculum Vitae

Ramiz Qudsi graduated from University of Delaware with a PhD. degree in Physics in 2021 under the guidance of Dr. Bennett Maruca. He is currently working as a Postdoctoral Associate at Center for Space Physics in Boston University. His research area focusses on space plasmas where he has published multiple papers on the interplay between microkinetic processes and turbulence. Check his Research page for more details on his work and his peer reviewed published papers.


He graduated from Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology in 2012 with a B.Tech degree in Physical Sciences and specialization in Astronomy and Astrophysics. He then worked at National Remote Sensing Center, Hyderabad as Scientist 'C' for 3 years before leaving to pursue his PhD. in Physics from University of Delaware.


When not busy sciencing, he loves to read and write. His favorite genre in literature is fantasy. J. R. R. Tolkien, Neil Gaiman and Brandon Sanderson are his absolute favorite authors. He likes writing poems and tiny tales. You can read some of them on his Blog. He is also an avid FC Barcelona and Indian cricket team fan.

Research

  •        Publications        Thesis        Presentations        Posters

Ramiz is currently doing research as a Postdoctoral Associate at Boston University with Dr. Brian Walsh. Main focus of his work is studying magnetic reconnections in Earth's magnetopause where he is working on refining predictions of reconnection X-lines in the Earth's magnetosphere using data from Magnetospheric Multiscale (MMS) Mission.


He is also involved in retrograde collisional analysis in solar wind, where he studies how the VDF of protons and heavier ions change because of collision as they travel from the Sun to 1 au.


Ramiz is also working on development of a sophisticated machine learning algorithm which will help in full 3-D reconstruction of interplanetary magnetic field using observation from 4 or more spacecraft in solar wind. The algorithm will work for an arbitrary number of spacecraft in a random orientation. Another machine learning project he is involved in attempts to find large-scale average trends in plasma parameters across the Heliosphere using data from 12-15 spacecraft.

Thesis


On the Interplay Between Microkinetics
and Turbulence in Space Plasmas


Abstract

Space plasmas in the inner heliosphere exist in a weakly collisional and turbulent state. Though energy transfer from large scales to smaller scales by turbulent cascade is widely accepted as an important feature of space plasmas, details of its exact dissipation process are lacking. Features arising because of turbulence, such as intermittency and temperature anisotropy, play important roles in the dynamics of space plasmas. Microkinetic linear instabilities induced by temperature anisotropy have been shown to change the statistical characteristics of plasma in a significant way.


Since the two processes, turbulence cascade and microkinetic instabilities, occur in the same physical and phase space, there is an interplay at work. In this study we investigated this interplay and the subsequent competition arising between the two, linear and nonlinear, processes. We found an explicit connection between intermittency and linear instability growth rates. We also showed localization of temperature enhancement regions along the intermittent structures, which in turn can trigger linear instabilities. Investigation of the two processes shed light on why linear theory works as well as it does, and shows the complicated nature of their interplay.


Information related to the exact spatial structure of the interplanetary magnetic field is vital to our understanding of the type of turbulence active in the space plasmas and the mechanism of turbulence cascade. This will help us discern the interplay between the two processes. We thus also report on a proof of concept study of magnetic field topology reconstruction using Gaussian Processes in machine learning.

Prose

Musings of an inconsequential life form over an ephemeral life!

With time every bond we share decays and every relation we form undergoes corrosion. Love fizzles away from our memory and as a direct consequence a melancholy feeling reigns over heart, momentarily. Though moments passes by imperceptibly the memories remain, some time despite our most vehement protest. Every beautiful memory associated with a broken bond or a corroded relation retains with it a quanta of emotion, more often than not, pain.



Ironically a relation at times is most stable during genesis, when its instability would not have mattered to you anyways. Once the process completes the expectations rise and renders it unstable. In due time it breaks apart and leaves you shattered and your life in turmoil.



An ephemeral life, the existence of which and its fading in the oblivion were merely one of the another uncountable and inconsequential inevitability of the Universe. Something which perturbs the grand equation of cosmos so infinitesimally that the Universe remains eternally oblivious to its existence or lack thereof.

And you realize that the life you have lived so far, with all its chaos and peace, agony and bliss and insignificance and grandeur is not only ephemeral, it is inconsequential!

Silence is the language of God, (all else is poor translation)

Rumi

A cryptic silence, unlike any mankind can decipher. So they have struggled over the ages to make sense out of it. Created a million different versions. Fought battles for millennia and still wage uncountable wars for supremacy and dominance. Innumerable heads beheaded for assumed heresy. Infinite souls lost in search of a finite myth. Passion caged for the fear of eternal damnation. Every innovation termed apostasy, every expression as act of dissent and nonconformity rewarded with execution. All these atrocities of followers and disciples of one version or another spread over centuries and yet the God spoke in but one language. An eternal silence. An indecipherable enigma for humanity. And so the barbarousness of believers continue, unchecked, unparalleled and inevitable!

That my dear man is my problem with a silent deity. For his existence or the lack of it matters not.

Tiny Tales

1

She was broken by the beliefs of a ridiculous society, and his heart by her submission to that force.

2

And then he watched his princess become a Queen. He was still a Prince.

3

She read somewhere, "If your love is true, let it be free. It will come back to you".

Decades later she realized her love was true, the proverb wasn't.

4

As the dessert arrived they discussed the sweetest thing each of them have ever tasted.

When his turn came he thought about her for a moment, smiled and said, "A pair of lips".

5

That day they all showered her with ridiculously expensive gifts. And yet nothing could compensate for her loss.

Dreams don't have a price tag.

6

A billionaire physicist, he looked at the final equation he has just written proving the relation between time and space, one very similar to Einstein's equation explaining mass and energy conversion. If only he could convert all the space he has into a moment with her. "sigh"

7

"What can I give to the man who has everything?" she thought hard and long.

While saying good bye she kissed him once and gave him a memory which will last forever (perhaps, she thought.)

8

As he adjusted his tie he remembered his obsession with her, her smile and the musical laughter and the pretty eyes just like he had for the last five years.

A second later he opened the door responding to the knock. She had a divine smile too.

9

A few days back he didn't have a second to spare. Today as he lay dying from the wounds of a lost battle, he had nothing but time. With a tinge of sadness he realized "sometimes an instant is forever, sometimes eternity is not long enough". All he wished was infinite instances for an eternity.

10

"Hey, nice bookmark".
Startled by the interruption he looked up to see two pretty lips curved in a smile still vibrating from inertia.
"Thanks", he said with a straight face and went back to his book.
Another potential love died uninitiated.

 

13

As he unpacked the last box and put the things in places he realized though his body arrived here a month back, the soul was yet to.

15

"Sup? By the way, I am in love."
"Congrats. Who is he?" He replied, half hoping it was a joke half hoping it was him.
Her reply broke his heart just a little, for the n th time, and terrified him. He knew, just like his this wasn't a story, it was a tragedy in making.

16

"I hope he loves you more than me" he said with a saddened smile.
"Didn't you say you love me infinitely? What could be more than infinity itself?" She said almost in an accusing tone.
"You know how some infinities are smaller than others? I hope my infinity was smaller one.
She disconnected the call and cried her heart out. That was her last conversation with him.

18

His was a soul trapped between two hearts. He couldn't let go of the one he had lost and knew he could never possibly win the other one he has fallen for.

19

He loved her a lot, she loved him back with almost equal intensity. And yet none of them were in love. That was how beautiful/sad their story was.

23

He thought distance would do the trick and hence moved as far from her as possible, to the other hemisphere of the world.
To his great dismay and relief he realized, unlike gravity memories and love do not follow the inverse square law.

24

He wished to be her first love. She desired to be his last.

In a way neither happy nor desired, both their wishes were granted.

26

Drunk, he lit another cigarette. Barely able to hold, he let it slid out of his fingers.
Next day the fire fighters tugged out his churned body from the debris. He had a smile playing on his burnt face.
"At least he died happy", thought the fireman.

27

2012: He signed up for NASA's mission to Mars.

2035: He stepped out of the Hab to -130 degrees outside and looked at the stars.
His sole consolation was, 225 million kilometers away no matter where she was she would be looking at the same constellations.

29

He asked her the permission to kiss her.
She said, "No!" with a smile on her lips.
60 years later when he saw her, he asked the same question.
Taking her silence for yes, he kissed her cold forehead and spread the pall over her face.
